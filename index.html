Memories of Spring ‚ù§Ô∏è

If I lit your heart with love, will you set my soul on fire? If I stick out my hands to you, will you plant a kiss on them? If the moon shades its light tonight, will you sit with me on the porch? I'll be roof over you in rainy days, duvet when cold shreds your bare body if you will stand by me now and always. I'll be the faithful guard at the door of your heart: never will any hurt get in to quench the sparkles in your eyes. Today, my Princess; tonight, my queen; tomorrow, my queen mother when I am under the seigh of your affection. I'll take you by the hand and lift your royal garment, for to be loved is to be seized within the boundaries of your care. You'll bring memories of springs and falls when in my bed we whisper and kiss. Like children: We'll dig in the dirt, blow bubbles, make dandelion wishes. We'll climb, swing, hide behind, dance around awakening trees. We'll paint backyards, playgrounds, beds With the colour of our skins. Then will our love grow, blossom and bloom in the spring.
